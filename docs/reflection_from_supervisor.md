# Reflection from supervisor

## What I did well

- I treated Wilmer as a proper colleague
- I took good care of Wilmer, always making sure he knew what to do
- I kept the promises I made

## What I could do better

- I am unsure if I should have talked more informally to Wilmer.
  I love my job and it _is_ a PRAO, so I tended to speak a lot about work. 
  I do not know how to decide what is the right amount of informal talk
- I am unsure how I handled talking about his -in my opinion- greatest
  weakness: giving an incorrect first impression, by showing too subtle
  emotions.

## My conclusion about my PRAO

- Wilmer is punctual:
  he always kept all his promises to meet at a specific place and time,
  typically being at least 15 minutes early
- Wilmer can work a full day: he worked the maximum amount of hours
  (which is 7 hours per day), without ever complaining about this.
  He admitted that school is easier (as school has less hours) :-)
- Wilmer is flexibel: he sometimes worked longer than the maximum amounts
  of hours on a day,
  so we had to schedule less hours on another day. This is quite normal at
  the place where I work and he was willing to go along with it
- Wilmer is patient, even on tasks that are frustrating (i.e. getting access
  to HPC clusters and other administrative tasks)
- Wilmer can focus and think: he was given tasks that require focus and
  thinking and he succeeded. He would not have been able to succeed
  without focus and deep thoughts.
  I never seen him being distracted by his phone.
- Wilmer can give a better first impression:
  he rarely shows his emotions when
  not asked for it and the emotions he does show are subtle.
  People that know him better see these subtle signs.
  I hope this subtlety will not hinder him too much in the future,
  as people want to know if he likes/dislikes something.
  Two people misinterpreted his non-sharing of
  emotions as being not interested in the job. I think such an
  incorrect first impression is unhelpful

In conclusion:

Wilmer was an excellent colleague. I hope he will help others more to see this
at a first glance. I will strongly recommend him for any job that requires
focus, deep thinking, patience and/or politeness.
